Computational Materials Science and Design

Computational Materials Design outlines a wide range of materials design technologies from first-principle calculations to continuum mechanics, with successful applications to materials design and development. Main goals include discovering new materials, determining materials behavior and mechanisms, explaining experiments, and exploring materials phenomena and theories. It is a rapidly growing multidisciplinary field that uses advanced computing capabilities to understand and solve complex problems. At its core it involves the development of models and simulations of natural systems. Materials simulation methods span all length scales, from electrons to components. Due to the balance of computational cost and predictive capability, density functional theory (DFT) has the most significant use in materials science. DFT most often refers to the calculation of the lowest energy state of the system, however, molecular dynamics (atomic motion through time) can be run with DFT computing forces between atoms.

Melting curve first-principles calculations of polymeric carbonic acid at high pressures by molecular dynamics approach

Computational Materials Discovery Laboratory, Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology, Moscow, Russia

https://uspex-team.org/en

MSc Thesis. Duration: Part Time 2 years

This work is devoted to the melting curve calculations of polymeric carbonic acid in the pressure range of 44 - 240 GPa by the method of statistical analysis of molecular-dynamic simulations of small-sized coexisting systems from first principles. For the first time, this method was applied to study organic compounds. Its advantages and disadvantages in relation to the specified substance are considered. The melting curve is obtained for a adequate number of (PV) points.

Prediction of new crystalline solid materials with low dielectric constant from first principles by evolutionary algorythm

Computational Materials Discovery Laboratory, Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology, Moscow, Russia

https://uspex-team.org/en

BSc Thesis. Duration: Part Time 1 year

Reaching the next level in any technological process requires new materials with new exceptional properties. Until recently, most new materials were discovered either incidentally or through trial and error that was unproductive and expensive. The rapid development of Computational Materials Science began only with the introduction of the evolutionary USPEX algorithm, one of the most powerful methods that can predict materials with any given and even extreme properties under various conditions. The main state-of-the-art algorithm incorporates “learning from history”, which is done through selection of the best structures to become parents of the new generation, survival of the fittest structures, and variation operators (i.e., recipes for producing child structures from parents). Acting upon good structures, variation operators lead, with high probability, to yet other better structures.

Nowadays microprocessors are used as a computational element in everything from the smallest recessed systems and mobile devices to enormous mainframes and supercomputers. The most important their property is the clock frequency (MHz). It characterizes the performance of a computer and indicates how many elementary operations a microprocessor runs in one second. Increase in the performance of integrated circuits (ICs) over the past few decades for the most part has been achieved due to increasing a transistor speed, decreasing its size and packaging more transistors on one chip. The factor that breaks this trend is a speed of slowing of the signal spread in a chip. Signal delays increase with each generation of scaling and limit the overall performance of an integrated circuit.

Dielectrics with a small dielectric constant are necessary to reduce mutual capacitances of conductors inside a chip, reducing the time constant of RC-chains and contributing the improvement of performance and the decrease of energy consumption. The purpose of this study, carried out by computational design methods was the prediction of new materials with a permittivity lower than that of currently used silicon dioxide SiO2 (ε = 4,0).

In the present work the molecular combination systems based on BeF2 and B2O3, (BeF2)x-(SiO2)y, (B2O3)x-(SiO2)y, were investigated. The USPEX algorithm combined with the quantum chemical calculation package VASP were used to predict crystal structures of isolators having a very small dielectric constant. To find the most optimal stoichiometry of a multicomponent compound the "variable composition" method was used. The number of atoms in a unit cell varied from 9 to 18. The first population of each calculation was randomly generated using a symmetry algorithm; second population structures were generated using evolutionary operators and the random symmetry algorithm in the following proportions: heredity operator (50%), softmutation operator (10%), permutation operator (10%) and random generation structures (30%). In subsequent generations a ratio of operators was chosen by the USPEX algorithm. To calculate local optimization in entalphy of a structure Density Functional Theory was used. To receive relaxed structures and their dielectric matrixes an approximate solution to the many-body Schrödinger equation was computed within Density Functional Theory. Interaction between ions and valence electrons was described by wave functionals with a maximum energy of 550 eV. In the Brillouin zone a uniform grid centered at Γ- point with a maximum resolution of 2π × 0.085 Å was constructed.

From each calculation structures with the lowest dielectric constant were selected. Then hardness was calculated and thermal stability with the coefficient of thermal expansion were estimated. Further the energy stability of these compounds was determined by comparing a structure enthalpy with the enthalpy of its components. Finally, a list of hard and energetically stable structures was compiled. New materials with a low dielectric constant, which can be potentially successfully employed in microelectronics to prevent large capacities, in the result of this work were predicted.
