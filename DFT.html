Computational materials science and engineering uses modeling, simulation, theory, and informatics to understand materials. Main goals include discovering new materials, determining material behavior and mechanisms, explaining experiments, and exploring materials theories. Prediction of new crystalline solid materials with low dielectric constant from first principles by evolutionary algorythm

Computational Materials Discovery Laboratory, Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology, Moscow, Russia

https://uspex-team.org/en

BSc Thesis. Duration: Part Time 1 year

Reaching the next level in any technological process requires new materials with new exceptional properties. Until recently, most new materials were discovered either incidentally or through trial and error that was unproductive and expensive. The rapid development of Computational Materials Science began only with the introduction of the evolutionary USPEX algorithm, one of the most powerful methods that can predict materials with any given and even extreme properties under various conditions. Profound interest in this area was the reason why a year ago I started my research in the Computational Materials Discovery Laboratory at MIPT. In my project I use our state-of-the-art algorithm to find new isolators with very low dielectric permittivity. I search them among different systems from two molecules with variable composition (until 20 atoms) per unit cell. To receive relaxed structures and their dielectric matrixes I compute an approximate solution to the many-body Schrödinger equation within Density Functional Theory. The main algorithm incorporates “learning from history”, which is done through selection of the best structures to become parents of the new generation, survival of the fittest structures, and variation operators (i.e., recipes for producing child structures from parents). Acting upon good structures, variation operators lead, with high probability, to yet other better structures. My study is very important to prevent large capacities in microelectronics.

Nowadays microprocessors are used as a computational element in everything from the smallest recessed systems and mobile devices to enormous mainframes and supercomputers. The most important their property is the clock frequency (MHz). It characterizes the performance of a computer and indicates how many elementary operations a microprocessor runs in one second. Increase in the performance of integrated circuits (ICs) over the past few decades for the most part has been achieved due to increasing a transistor speed, decreasing its size and packaging more transistors on one chip (Fig.1). The factor that breaks this trend is a speed of slowing of the signal spread in a chip. Signal delays increase with each generation of scaling and limit the overall performance of an integrated circuit.

Dielectrics with a small dielectric constant [1-3] are necessary to reduce mutual capacitances of conductors inside a chip, reducing the time constant of RC-chains and contributing the improvement of performance and the decrease of energy consumption. The purpose of this study, carried out by computational design methods was the prediction of new materials with a permittivity lower than that of currently used silicon dioxide SiO2 (ε = 4,0).

In the present work the systems based on BeF2, (BeF2)x(SiO2)y, (B2O3)x(SiO2)y were investigated. The USPEX algorithm combined with the quantum chemical calculation package VASP were used to predict crystal structures having a very small dielectric constant. To find the most optimal stoichiometry of a multicomponent compound the "variable composition" method was used. The number of atoms in a unit cell varied from 9 to 18. The first population of each calculation was randomly generated using a symmetry algorithm; second population structures were generated using evolutionary operators and the random symmetry algorithm in the following proportions: heredity operator (50%), softmutation operator (10%), permutation operator (10%) and random generation structures (30%). In subsequent generations a ratio of operators was chosen by the USPEX algorithm. To calculate local optimization in entalphy of a structure Density Functional Theory was used [4]. Interaction between ions and valence electrons was described by wave functionals with a maximum energy of 550 eV. In the Brillouin zone a uniform grid centered at Γ- point with a maximum resolution of 2π × 0.085 Å was constructed.

From each calculation structures with the lowest dielectric constant were selected. Then hardness was calculated and thermal stability with the coefficient of thermal expansion were estimated. Further the energy stability of these compounds was determined by comparing a structure enthalpy with the enthalpy of its components. Finally, a list of hard and energetically stable structures was compiled. New materials with a low dielectric constant, which can be successfully employed in micro- and nanoelectronics, in the result of this work were predicted.
