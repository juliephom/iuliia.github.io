Poly(vinylidene fluoride) foams for enhanced airborne sound absorption

Actuators and Transducers Laboratory, Institute of Materials Research and Engineering (IMRE), A*STAR, Singapore

2nd Year MSc Thesis 2018-2019. Duration: 6 months

*Agency for Science, Technology and Research (A*STAR) is a statutory board under the Ministry of Trade and Industry of Singapore. A*STAR's Institute of Materials Research and Engineering (IMRE) is a part of this Science and Engineering Research Council with a unique combination of R&D capabilities, commercial partnerships and strategic alliances, where comprehensive materials-related solutions for global and local partners, that lead to new commercial products, are developed. IMRE is heavily engaged in developing advanced functional materials for various sensing application as one of the major R&D area (https://www.a-star.edu.sg/imre).

The goal of this research topic is to develop noise mitigation solutions. Noise pollution is becoming an important issue especially in big cities. Sound absorbing materials help to convert mechanical energy of sound waves into heat through the noise/vibration absorption function. Polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) foams show the superior sound absorption performance over a wide frequency range. However, their production cost still remains highly uncompetitive to polyurethane (PU) foams, which makes their commercial implementation obstructing. Low cost fabrication method of PVDF foams was developed in the lab. Acoustic properties, electomechanical response and microscopic structure are studied in this work.
