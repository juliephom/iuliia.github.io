Research Area of Interests

Iuliia's research areas cover smart materials with sensing, actuation, energy harvesting and storage functions, and their applications as sensors, actuators and transducers. The materials studied through his current work include ferroic, piezoelectric, dielectric, photovoltaic, chemical and bio sensing materials. He is interested in understanding and improving the structure and properties of these smart materials, and further exploring their signal/energy conversion and storage functional mechanisms including their potential device applications. As the program manager, he is leading a research program focusing on material-critical sensor and transducers at Institute of Materials Research and Engineering in A*STAR. Working together with his colleagues and students, he has designed and demonstrated a variety of material-critical sensors, actuators, and transducers on the basis of the functional mechanisms of the smart materials. Processing of functional thin films, thick films, multilayers, and micro/nano fabrication processes are often applied to produce miniaturized devices.

Research Projects

Poly(vinylidene fluoride) foams for enhanced airborne sound absorption

Institute of Materials Research and Engineering (IMRE), A*STAR, Singapore

May 2019

The goal of this research topic is to develop noise mitigation solutions. Noise pollution became an important issue especially in big cities. Sound absorbing materials help to convert mechanical energy of sound waves into heat through the noise/vibration absorption function. Nowadays, polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) foams show the superior sound absorption performance over a wide frequency range. However, their production cost still remains highly uncompetitive to polyurethane (PU) foams, which makes their commercial implementation obstructing. Low cost fabrication method of PVDF foams was developed in the lab. Acoustic properties, electomechanical response and microscopic structure are studied in this work.
